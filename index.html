About

My name is Shantanu Parmar, and I am an undergraduate Engineering student pursuing a Bachelor's in Technology in Information and Communication Technology at Marwadi University. I am an American Physical Society Matching Membership Fellow and a member of the Inspired 24 Space Initiative. My research interests lie at the intersection of Astrophysics, Machine Learning, and VLSI. In my free time, I enjoy astrophotography, hiking, and building electronics projects.

Research

Full list of publications on arXiv, Google Scholar, ORCID

Astrophysics

A Comparative Evaluation of Transitional and Full Protoplanetary Disks

March 2025 – Present | Supervisor: Dr. Charles J Law

Imaged and analyzed Submillimeter Array observations of a sample of transition disks. Developed classification metrics using SED fitting and machine learning. Findings reveal faster evolution in transitional disks due to planet formation gaps.

OmniFormer: Context-aware transformer localizing transient noise sources in LIGO

Nov 2024 – Present | Supervisor: Dr. Marco Cavaglia

Designed a transformer model to source transient noise origins in advanced LIGO systems. Tested on a curated dataset of 1.2B samples over 12 parameters for the O3a data run.

GitHub

Deep Learning & AI/ML

StrCGAN: A Generative Framework for Stellar Image Restoration WACV 2026

Jan – Sep 2025

Developed a Cyclic GAN optimized for astronomy with 3D convolutions. Targets low-SNR datasets from NIR to optical wavelengths. Achieved 30% PSNR improvement over baselines on all-sky survey data.

arXiv:2509.19805 | GitHub | Website

Under Review

Benchmarking Deep Learning Object Detection on Feature-deficient Dataset CAIP 2025

Dec 2024 – May 2025

Created 10,000+ labeled astrophotography dataset under poor conditions. Benchmarked YOLO, RetinaNet, EfficientDet. YOLOv8 outperforms in low-light with public Zenodo release.

arXiv:2508.06537 | Dataset (Zenodo)
